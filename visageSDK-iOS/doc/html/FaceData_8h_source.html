280  /* Translation of the head, compensated.
281  * <i>This variable is set only while tracker is tracking (TRACK_STAT_OK).</i>
282  *
283  * Translation is expressed with three coordinates x, y, z.
284  * The coordinate system is such that when looking towards the camera, the direction of x is to the
285  * left, y iz up, and z points towards the viewer. The global origin (0,0,0) is placed at the camera.
286  *
287  * Translation of the head relative to its position in the first video frame.
288  * This is compensated translation estimated by the tracker.
289  * Compensated translation values take into account the fact that the tracker
290  * uses a relatively flat face model for tracking, so the center of rotation of this model
291  * is in the front area of the head, while the anatomical center
292  * of rotation is behind, in the base of the neck. Therefore, when the rotation
293  * is applied to a 3D head model with anatomically correct center of rotation, the
294  * face naturally translates as well. When this translation is compounded with
295  * the translation values obtained from the tracker, the total resulting translation
296  * is exaggerated. To avoid this exaggerated translation of the animated head,
297  * the translation can be compensated. The compensation algorithm
298  * estimates how much the translation would be exaggerated, and makes it that much
299  * smaller. The compensated translation can directly be applied to animated head
300  * models that use the neck base as the center of rotation, and is expected to give
301  * better results than the uncompensated translation.
302  *
303  * @see faceTranslation, faceRotation
304  */
305  float faceTranslationCompensated[3];
